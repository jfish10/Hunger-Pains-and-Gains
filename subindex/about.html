Random Facts About Me in List Form


  1. I try my best to spend time with family and friends and maintain a positive outlook on life.
  2. I'm studying computer science to reach my goal of becoming a software engineer.
  3. My favorite instrument in the world is the saxophone, but I haven't played it in years (I'd love to get back to playing jazz music again).
  4. I'm a news junkie. I love loading up on any and all news related to foreign policy, economics, sports, global affairs/security, technology, and more.
  5. My go-to sources for news are the New York Times, The Atlantic, Huffington Post, Business Insider, Gizmodo, Wired, The Economist, Wall Street Journal, ESPN, SilverScreenAndRoll, Deadspin, Lifehacker, Reddit, Rolling Stone, and The New Yorker.
  6. Sometimes, as evidenced above, I have a habit of reading news articles for hours instead of going out and simply living life.
  7. My favorite programming language is Java. Something about the language makes it extremely beautiful and exciting to use.
  8. My day-to-day involves working in SQL and Excel. I love everything about databases and data and if I could have a career working with SQL, I'd jump on it without hesitation.
  9. A chain restaurant that I can't get enough of is Buca Di Beppo. I think it's because of the large portions, but either way, I'm obsessed.
  10. My favorite sport in the world is basketball, and I'm a die-hard Lakers fan, ever since the Nick Van Excel/Eddie Jones years. They'll be back on top soon enough.
  11. I'm not a fan of horror movies (hate them), but I'll watch them occasionally just to give my courage a bit of a pop-quiz, so to speak.
  12. Sushi and Mexican food are the only foods I couldn't fathom in a million years living without.
  13. Prior to graduating from college, I took a year off to write for Business Insider (as well as write for Affairs Today, a global student-run news site that had a good readership but is now unfortunately defunct).
  14. If I retired today, it would be because I wanted to read and write books, articles, and stories for the rest of my life.

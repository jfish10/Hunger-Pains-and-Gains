Random Facts About Me in List Form


  1. I try my best to spend time with family and friends and maintain a positive outlook on life.
  2. I've fallen in love with everything data structures and problem solving. I may be getting stuck on LC easy's right now, but ask me how I'm doing 1 year from now ;).
  3. My favorite instrument in the world is the saxophone, but I haven't played it in years (I'd love to get back to playing jazz music again).
  4. I'm a news junkie - I love loading up on any and all news related to foreign policy, economics, sports, global affairs/security, technology, and more.
  5. My go-to sources for news are the New York Times, The Atlantic, Huffington Post, Business Insider, Gizmodo, Wired, The Economist, Wall Street Journal, ESPN, SilverScreenAndRoll, Deadspin, Lifehacker, Reddit, Rolling Stone, and The New Yorker.
  6. Sometimes, as evidenced above, I have a habit of reading news articles for hours instead of going out and simply living life.
  7. My favorite programming language is Java. It may be verbose and at times clunky (who doesn't love generics?), but as someone who uses a mechanical keyboard and loves the sound of the clicking and clacking of keys, Java can't be beat.
  8. My day-to-day involves working in SQL and Excel. I love everything about databases and data and if I could have a career working purely with SQL, I'd jump on it without hesitation.
  9. One of my favorite restaurants is Buca Di Beppo. Large portions, white cheese pizza, and complimentary bread - it can't be beat.
  10. My favorite sport in the world is basketball, and I'm a die-hard Lakers fan, ever since the Kobe-Shaq championship years. Can't wait to see the Lebron-AD pick and rolls this year.
  11. I'm not a fan of horror movies (hate them), but I'll watch them occasionally just to give my courage a bit of a pop-quiz, so to speak.
  12. My favorite food in the world is ramen, followed by soba and bean and cheese burritos. The simple life is often the best life.
  13. Prior to graduating from college, I took a year off to write for Business Insider (as well as write for Affairs Today, a global student-run news site that had a good readership but is now unfortunately defunct).
  14. Favorite type of tea? Green tea, hands down.
  15. After I retire, I would love to spend the rest of my life reading and writing as many books, articles, and stories as time will allow.
